Oil

Engine oil is the most popular product in servicing your ride, and choosing the right or wrong ones will be the most vital decision for keeping your ride safe and strong.

For most of the motorcycle that are below 175cc in displacement, the viscosity rating should be around 30 to 40 for newly bought bikes and between 40 to 50 for bike that have ran over 50,000km in total

There are 3 most popular type of oils: Mineral, Semi-Synthetics (Synthetic Blended, Blended) and Fully Synthetics. For best economy, you would want to go with Semi-Synthetics since it is cheaper than the Full Synthetics but performs better than the Mineral. But if you are a performance enthusiast, then you should always go for Full Synthetics, with Ester grade if available, since they are the best in performance but also are pretty expensive.

Suggested

Recommended brands and models are: Motul 300V, Motul 7100, Eni i-Ride MOTO, Liqui Moly Motorbike.



Coolants

Coolant, though is not replaced very frequently is still a very important thing that you should give attention on after a while of riding. It is the only thing to help circulate the heat from your engine to the radiator, keeping the engine from over heat and keeping you comfortable when you ride.

Coolant have two main type based on their origin product: Propylene Glycol and Ethylene Glycol, there is also another type that are widely used for Trackday that is water based coolant with little to none glycol additives.

Choosing the coolant for your ride, you should always go for propylene glycol that have no silicates or phosphates additives to ensure best heat extrction and radiator proection. If you want to go for a Trackday, choose the one that does not have any glycol in it.

Suggested

Recommended brand and models: Motul Motocool, Bel-ray Motochill, Engine Ice (Track use), Castrol Motorcycle Coolant.



Brake Fluids

Brake fluid is the thing that will determine whether you will be safe or not on the road, so always pay high attention to when you should change it.

Brake fluid have a tendency to absorb moisture from the air so always use moderate amount and seal packages so that you won't waste fluids and money. Always pay close attention to the guiding of your manual to choose the right type of fluids, in our case with the M-Slaz, it will be DOT3 or DOT4

Suggested

Recommended brand and model: Motul, Liqui Moly, Castrol



Brake Pads

Brake pad is the next most important thing to consider during a brake sevice, since it is what gives you friction and stops you from hitting what is in front of you.

For casual riding and commuting, using simple orgainc pads is enough for you, but for maximum safety during normal commuting and long trip, using a sintered or a compounded pad will be better for both mileage and performance.

Suggested

Recommended brand and model: EBC Brake Pad, Organic ECE R90 pads, V-pads Semi-Sintered (for best economy), H-pads Sintered (best braking performance)



Spark Plugs

Spark plug though is not a very high maintenace part, still, after a while, you should have a check to see if it needed any fixes or replacements, if you want to, you can upgrade it to a better spark plug.

Our M-Slaz uses a NGK CR9E spark plug, so you should have a base to consider which one to upgrade to.

Suggested

Recommended brand and model: Have a look at the many Iridium spark plugs from NGK or Denso



Bearings

Bearing that includes wheel bearings, steering head bearing, engine bearing, clutch pack bearing are also very important if you want to keep your ride running smooth, with little to no noise, and run strong for a long time.

If there are any signs of wear and tear, then you should inspect for dimension and specs of the bearing to be able to find an appropriate replacement.

Suggested

Recommended brand and model: Nachi Bearing, SKF Bearings.
